Compare two algorithms

The graph presents a direct comparison between two recommender system algorithms across multiple datasets using the evaluations of a selected performance metric such as nDCG, Hit Ratio or Recall with a K-value. The results are further divided into five distinct regions to provide deeper insights into their comparative performance.

  • The red area identifies datasets where both algorithms struggle.
  • The green area identifies datasets where both algorithms perform well.
  • The blue and yellow areas identify datasets where only one algorithm outperforms the other.
  • The white area identifies datasets where both algorithms perform moderately.
By dividing the results into these regions, the graph gives detailed information of where each algorithm succeeds or fails relative to the other. This helps identify specific datasets that are solved by one algorithm over the other. However, since the comparison involves only two algorithms, conclusions about overall dataset difficulty or general algorithm suitability need be evaluated carefully, as other algorithms may behave differently. For that, Algorithm Search Space (APS) is recommended.
